So what are the skills, qualifications and experience a Software Engineer needs?

Software engineers require a Bachelor Degree in a related field such as:

  • Bachelor of Computer Science or Software Engineering

  • If an alternate degree is obtained such as a Bachelor of Information Technology, a career in software engineering is still possible however, pursuing a graduate certificate/diploma in Computer Science or Software Engineering may provide additional skills and knowledge that you won’t get from a Bachelor of Information Technology.

  • To take your career even further, the individual can obtain a Masters of Engineering (software) or Computer Science

Software engineers must have effective communication as they generally work within a team so they will need to poses excellent time management, be passionate and high-end user focus, and be logical and able to problem solving. Software engineers must be proficient in programming languages, development technologies and web development tools.

Becoming a Software Engineer can take roughly 4+ years to acquire a junior level position.

Introducing Dr Queue

I would like to create a software for the healthcare industry and an application for patients.

A bit of background, four years ago I had open heart surgery and a month prior to the surgery, I had so many appointments with different doctors and specialists, along with many tests consisting of various scans and pathology.

I was still working full-time but I spent so much time waiting around to see my doctor or to get a test done. Most of these appointments/tests can only be done during the working week. I was really fortunate to work for a company that was understanding of the position I was in and that it didn’t affect my employment. Still to this day I have regular follow ups and require different testing and consultations which means I still spend time waiting around. I’ve also spent my fair share of time in hospital.

All this time I have spent waiting around to see my doctors and specialists, in the hospital, getting pathology tests done, waiting to get scans done.. This is time that could be better utilized, won’t affect my job (now that I am in a more senior role) and will allow me to balance my life.

Imagine we had an effective, user-friendly software for the healthcare industry that would translate information to an application for patients (of course, super user-friendly) that would allow me to plan ahead and better yet, receive reminders and notifications.
